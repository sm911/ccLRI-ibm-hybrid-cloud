Transforming Biomedical Discovery: An IBM Hybrid Cloud Architecture for the Lerner Research Institute

Jim Venuto

October 2025

 

Introduction: A Partnership to Accelerate Biomedical Discovery

The Cleveland Clinic's Lerner Research Institute (LRI) operates at the intersection of biomedical innovation and computational infrastructure. As research becomes increasingly data-intensive and dependent on AI and machine learning, traditional infrastructure models are inadequate to support the scale, speed, and security required for contemporary discovery.

This document introduces an IBM Hybrid Cloud architecture designed to address the technical constraints that limit research velocity. Developed under the landmark 10-year Discovery Accelerator partnership between IBM and Cleveland Clinic, this architecture enables LRI scientists to focus on hypothesis testing and innovation rather than infrastructure management.

The timing is significant. Advances in generative AI, cloud-native automation, and secure data collaboration are reshaping research infrastructure requirements. IDC (International Data Corporation) projects that global spending on "GenAI solutions" will reach $151.1 billion by 2027. This forecast implies a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 85.9% from 2023 to 2027. underscoring the importance for institutions to modernize their platforms. This paper provides a blueprint for doing sointegrating on-premises assets with IBM Cloud to create a secure, elastic, and adaptable research environment.

Executive Summary

This document outlines a strategic and technical framework for transforming biomedical research at LRI through a hybrid-by-design IBM Cloud architecture. The solution directly addresses four critical challenges:

         GPU bottlenecks that delay AI model training and inference.

         Petabyte-scale data management inefficiencies that hinder collaboration and inflate costs.

         Stringent compliance requirements for Protected Health Information (PHI).

         Operational overhead that limits research agility and self-service.

IBM's architecture responds with five integrated pillars:

         Unified Platform & Connectivity: Red Hat OpenShift, IBM Cloud Satellite, and Direct Link enable workload portability and secure data locality.

         Elastic Compute Layer: On-demand access to high-performance GPUs (NVIDIA H100, Intel Gaudi 3, AMD MI300X) supports scalable AI workloads.

         Intelligent Data Fabric: IBM Storage Scale virtualizes storage across tiers, automating lifecycle management and reducing TCO.

         Security & Compliance by Design: FIPS 140-2 Level 4 HSMs, KYOK encryption, and continuous compliance monitoring ensure governance.

         AI-Driven Optimization: IBM Turbonomic and Cloudability automate resource allocation and financial oversight.

An 18-month phased implementation roadmap ensures measurable outcomes, including reduced provisioning time, improved GPU utilization, and accelerated time-to-insight. This architecture positions LRI to adopt future innovations such as federated research and quantum computingadvancing Cleveland Clinic's mission to lead in life sciences discovery.

1. The Research Computing Imperative: LRI's Core Challenges

Effective architecture must be grounded in solving the real-world operational and computational bottlenecks that impede research. Understanding the specific challenges faced by the Lerner Research Institute is therefore the essential first step in designing a solution that delivers measurable value. The following core challenges represent significant constraints on the speed and scale of discovery at LRI:

Intensive AI & GPU Demand

AI-driven research, particularly deep learning on medical images and genomic data, requires substantial computational power from specialized GPUs. The limited capacity of on-premises GPU clusters creates a significant bottleneck, leading to long job queues for researchers. This directly delays project timelines, resulting in the underutilization of LRI's scientific talent.

Petabyte-Scale Data & Storage Management

LRI generates massive and rapidly growing volumes of data from sources like genomic sequencing and high-resolution medical imaging. Managing this petabyte-scale data with traditional storage systems is inefficient and costly. These systems struggle to scale, leading to data silos that hinder collaboration and require redundant data copies. The high total cost of ownership (TCO) for performance-oriented on-premises storage strains research budgets that could otherwise be allocated to science.

Stringent Security, Compliance & Data Governance

As a healthcare research entity, LRI handles Protected Health Information (PHI) subject to rigorous HIPAA regulations and other data privacy requirements. Ensuring end-to-end compliance within a complex research computing environment is a significant operational burden. Any cloud adoption strategy must provide robust data privacy and governance, turning compliance from a manual, periodic effort into a continuous, automated posture.

Operational Overhead & Research Agility

The dependency on central IT for provisioning compute, storage, and software environments creates friction and introduces delays. The months-long procurement and setup cycles for on-premises hardware are misaligned with the dynamic nature of modern research. This operational model hinders the ability of researchers to rapidly test hypotheses, scale experiments, or pivot to new lines of inquiry, ultimately limiting scientific agility.

These challenges collectively create an environment where technology, rather than enabling research, becomes a constraint. The proposed IBM Hybrid Cloud architecture is designed to systematically dismantle these barriers.

2. The IBM Hybrid Cloud Architectural Framework

The IBM Hybrid Cloud solution is a unified, automated platform that extends LRI's on-premises capabilities into the secure, scalable IBM Cloud. The architecture is "hybrid-by-design," engineered to integrate with and enhance existing systems, not replace them. This framework is built upon five core pillars that work in concert to deliver a seamless, intelligent, and governed research environment.

2.1. Foundational Layer: Unified Platform & Connectivity

This foundational layer creates a single, consistent operational environment that spans LRI's data centers and the IBM public cloud, eliminating the silos that complicate traditional IT. Key components include:

         Red Hat OpenShift: Serves as the consistent container platform that creates a "common namespace" across all environments. This enables true workload portability, allowing researchers and developers to adopt a "develop once, deploy anywhere" model. Applications can be seamlessly moved to the locationon-premises or in the cloudthat best suits performance, data locality, or cost requirements.

         IBM Cloud Satellite: Extends the unified IBM Cloud control plane directly into LRI's on-premises environment. This technology allows secure IBM Cloud services to run behind the hospital firewall, respecting strict data locality and sovereignty requirements for sensitive PHI while still benefiting from cloud-native automation and management.

         IBM Cloud Direct Link: Establishes a private, secure, and high-bandwidth fiber connection between LRI's data centers and the IBM Cloud. This dedicated link is essential for enabling low-latency data flow, allowing for the seamless transfer of large datasets (e.g., medical images or genomic sequences) between on-premises storage and cloud-based compute.

Collectively, these technologies establish an open, hybrid architecture that extends IBM Cloud's capabilities wherever LRI needs them. IBM Cloud's platform is recognized as a highly open and secure public cloud for business. This open approach ensures LRI isn't locked into a single vendor's ecosystem and can uphold strict data locality requirementsan approach distinct from other clouds that often impose proprietary frameworks.

2.2. Elastic Compute Layer: On-Demand GPU Cloud Bursting

This layer provides on-demand, self-service access to computational resources, directly addressing the on-premises GPU capacity bottleneck. The architecture provides access to a diverse portfolio of GPU accelerators on IBM Cloudincluding the latest NVIDIA, Intel, and AMD optionsensuring the optimal hardware is available for any specific research task.

IBM Cloud's GPU portfolio provides substantial options. It was the first cloud service provider to offer Intel's Gaudi 3 AI accelerators to customers, and under a multi-year partnership with AMD and Zyphra, IBM has deployed one of the largest generative AI training clusters on IBM Cloud using AMD Instinct MI300X GPUs. This breadth of choice ensures that each research workload can be matched to an optimal accelerator, maximizing performance and cost-efficiency.

This on-demand availability is coupled with automated, elastic scaling. The platform utilizes a cloud bursting model where on-premises workloads can automatically access public cloud resources during peak demand. This directly contrasts the months-long procurement and setup cycles for on-premises hardware with the ability to provision GPU resources in minutes on the cloud. Orchestration tools like Ray or enterprise schedulers like IBM Spectrum LSF can be configured to provision and de-provision expensive GPU resources only for the duration of a job. This model eliminates massive upfront capital expenditures (CapEx) on hardware that may sit idle, converting it into a predictable and efficient operational expense (OpEx).

2.3. Data Fabric Layer: Intelligent Tiered Storage Management

This layer provides a solution for managing massive research data volumes efficiently and cost-effectively, breaking down data silos and reducing storage TCO.

The core technology is IBM Storage Scale (formerly known as IBM Spectrum Scale), which creates a unified data repository. It virtualizes all of LRI's storage resourcesfrom high-performance on-premises flash and disk (including existing NAS/SAN systems) to virtually limitless cloud object storageinto a single, logical file system accessible from anywhere.

         Policy-Driven Automated Tiering: Information Lifecycle Management (ILM) policies automatically and transparently move data between storage tiers based on access patterns, age, and project status. Frequently accessed data for active research resides on high-performance NVMe flash, while infrequently accessed data is moved to the low-cost, durable IBM Cloud Object Storage tier, dramatically reducing storage TCO.

         Transparent Researcher Access: This entire process is seamless for researchers. They access all data through the common namespace using standard protocols (e.g., POSIX/NFS) without needing to know or manage its physical location. This simplifies data access and eliminates the need for manual data movement.

         High-Performance I/O: Advanced features like intelligent caching and data replication ensure that data is available with local-like performance for compute-intensive jobs. For example, when a cloud-based GPU needs a dataset, Storage Scale can automatically cache the relevant files in the cloud, ensuring compute resources are never starved for data.

2.4. Security & Compliance Layer: Governance by Design

Security and compliance are not afterthoughts but are embedded into every layer of architecture. This "governance-by-design" approach proactively manages risk and ensures the protection of sensitive health data.

         HIPAA-Ready Platform: IBM Cloud is a HIPAA-ready platform, and IBM provides a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) to support Cleveland Clinic's compliance obligations for handling PHI.

         Advanced Data Encryption: The platform features comprehensive data protection capabilities. This includes FIPS 140-2 Level 4 certified Hardware Security Modules (HSMs)the highest level of commercial certification availableand the Keep Your Own Key (KYOK) feature. IBM Cloud's HSM offering is distinguished as the only public cloud HSM designed to meet FIPS 140-2 Level 4 security requirements. (Most competing providers offer HSMs only up to Level 3.) This gives LRI hardware-enforced assurance that its encryption keys are protected against compromise, and KYOK ensures that Cleveland Clinic retains sole, exclusive control over its data encryption keys.

         Continuous Compliance Automation: The IBM Cloud Security and Compliance Center (SCC) provide a unified dashboard for managing the institution's security and compliance posture across the hybrid environment. It continuously monitors for configuration drift against frameworks like HIPAA and NIST, provides automated alerts, and simplifies audit preparation by automatically collecting and organizing evidence.

2.5. Optimization & Management Layer: AI-Driven Automation

This layer leverages automation to ensure the hybrid environment runs optimally from both a performance and a financial perspective, minimizing waste and manual effort.

         IBM Turbonomic: This Application Resource Management (ARM) tool continuously analyzes application resource demand and matches it to the underlying infrastructure supply. It automates critical actions like rightsizing virtual machines, scaling container resources, and making intelligent, data-driven decisions on when to burst workloads to the cloud. This assures application performance while simultaneously eliminating over-provisioning and infrastructure waste. (This model is documented in healthcare deployments; for example, Providence Health leveraged Turbonomic to optimize 1,900 workloads during a major cloud migration, realizing over $2 million in savings while assuring the performance of critical applications during COVID-19 surges.)

         IBM Cloudability: Serves as the extensive dashboard for the hybrid environment. Sourced from Apptio (an IBM company), it provides granular visibility into costs by researcher, project, or department. Cloudability enables budget controls, anomaly detection alerts, and recommendations to reduce spending. According to Apptio, this level of FinOps governance can reduce cloud unit costs by 30% or more by eliminating waste and maximizing commitment coverage, ensuring complete financial oversight and eliminating the risk of budget surprises.

Together, these five pillars form an integrated, intelligent, and secure platform that directly addresses LRI's core challenges and creates a foundation for accelerated research.

3. Architectural Blueprints in Action: Operational Flows

This section moves from architectural theory to operational reality, detailing how these integrated components automate complex decisions and streamline research data pipelines to deliver tangible efficiency.

3.1. The Automated Workload & Resource Optimization Flow

When a researcher submits a workload, an intelligent and automated decision-making process is initiated to ensure optimal placement and resource allocation without human intervention:

         Workload Classification: The system ingests the job request and automatically classifies its type (e.g., AI training, genomic analysis, medical imaging inference) and its specific resource requirements (GPU type, CPU, RAM, storage I/O).

         Resource & Policy Assessment: The platform assesses the current availability of on-premises resources and evaluates the request against a set of predefined policies governing data locality (for example, PHI must remain on-premises), security, compliance, and cost.

         Automated Resource Selection: Based on this multi-faceted assessment, the system makes an optimal allocation decision. For example, it may select a cloud-based NVIDIA H100 GPU for a large training job, assign its data to the high-performance storage tier, and automatically apply the necessary security and encryption policies (like KYOK).

         Continuous Optimization: Once the workload is running, IBM Turbonomic and the Security and Compliance Center continuously monitor it. Turbonomic ensures the application always has the resources it needs to perform, while the SCC validates that it remains compliant with all security policiescreating a self-tuning, self-governing environment.

3.2. The End-to-End Research Data Pipeline

This flow details the complete lifecycle of research data as it moves through the hybrid cloud platform, from initial acquisition to the generation of scientific insight:

         Ingest & Acquisition: Data from diverse sourcessuch as imaging devices (PACS), genomic sequencers (LIMS), wearables, and Electronic Health Record (EHR) systemsis ingested into the platform. At this initial point, PHI can be de-identified or tokenized as required by data governance policies to ensure privacy.

         Multi-Zone Data Lakehouse: Data is managed within distinct, policy-governed zones in the unified IBM Storage Scale and Cloud Object Storage environment. This includes a Landing Zone for raw, unmodified data; a Refined Zone for cleansed, processed, and analysis-ready data; and an Archive Zone for long-term, immutable retention to meet regulatory and compliance requirements.

         Flexible & Governed Compute: Containerized data processing pipelines running on Red Hat OpenShift access the data. These pipelines can dynamically leverage different types of compute resources as neededfor example, using a powerful NVIDIA H100 for initial model training and a more cost-effective NVIDIA L40S for subsequent batch inference jobs. All compute actions are governed by the platform's integrated security framework, including KYOK encryption and SCC policy enforcement.

         Outputs & Collaboration: The final outputs of the researchsuch as trained AI models, interactive dashboards, and shareable data productsare stored in a model registry or made available via secure APIs. This enables seamless and governed collaboration between researchers and departments, all under the platform's unified security and data governance umbrella.

4. Phased Implementation Roadmap

The adoption of this architecture would be planned as a strategic, multi-phase journey designed to deliver immediate value, mitigate risk, and build momentum. This 18-month roadmap ensures a successful, measurable, and ROI-driven implementation.

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 06)

This phase focuses on establishing the core infrastructure and secure connectivity between LRI's on-premises environment and the IBM Cloud.

         Establish secure, high-bandwidth connectivity via IBM Cloud Direct Link.

         Deploy managed Red Hat OpenShift clusters with integrated Identity and Access Management (IAM) and Keep Your Own Key (KYOK) encryption.

         Implement IBM Storage Scale and configure initial policies for tiering data to IBM Cloud Object Storage.

         Onboard the environment into the IBM Cloud Security and Compliance Center for baseline monitoring, and deploy IBM Turbonomic in observe mode.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): GPU Provision Time ↓, Compliance Drift Findings ↓.

Phase 2: Scale (Months 612)

This phase focuses on migrating pilot workloads to demonstrate value and activating advanced automation and optimization capabilities.

         Roll out pilot GPU bursting workloads for key research areas like bioimaging and genomics.

         Activate automated resource optimization with IBM Turbonomic.

         Implement budget controls with IBM Cloudability.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): GPU Queue Time ↓, $/Training Run ↓, $/TB-Month Storage ↓, % Controls Automated ↑.

Phase 3: Optimize (Months 1218)

This phase focuses on scaling the platform to an enterprise-wide service, enabling research self-service, and implementing advanced automation.

         Transition to a multi-tenant, self-service model for researchers to provision their own environments.

         Implement advanced "policy-as-code" automation for security and operational governance.

         Explore and pilot federated collaboration patterns for secure, multi-institutional research projects.

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs): 90th Percentile Wait Time ↓, GPU Utilization ↑, Audit Prep Time ↓, Cumulative Cost Savings ↑.

This phased approach ensures that each stage of the implementation delivers tangible benefits and builds a solid foundation for the next, guaranteeing a smooth and successful transformation.

5. Conclusion: A Future-Ready Platform for Biomedical Discovery

The proposed IBM Hybrid Cloud solution is more than an infrastructure upgrade; it is a strategic platform designed to remove technological constraints from the researchers at the Lerner Research Institute. By seamlessly integrating LRI's on-premises assets with the elastic, secure, and automated capabilities of the IBM Cloud, this architecture directly addresses the core challenges of modern biomedical research, creating an environment where science can move at the speed of insight.

The primary architectural advantages deliver substantial value to LRI and to the broader Cleveland Clinic mission:

         Accelerated Time-to-Science: Providing researchers with self-service, on-demand access to computational resources, eliminating queues and accelerating the journey from hypothesis to insight.

         Intelligent Automation & Efficiency: Reducing routine administrative tasks for LRI's researchers and IT specialists, allowing them to focus entirely on innovation and scientific breakthroughs.

         Robust Security & Compliance: Protecting Cleveland Clinic's most sensitive health data and its reputation with all-encompassing, hardware-enforced encryption and continuous, automated governance.

         A Foundation for Future Innovation: Creating a flexible, scalable, and secure platform that positions LRI to harness the next generation of technologies (including quantum computing) as part of the ongoing Discovery Accelerator partnership.

In summary, IBM Cloud's capabilities are well-suited for LRI's needs. It offers an open, hybrid architecture with comprehensive security featuresIBM Cloud is recognized as a highly open and secure public cloud for business. Capabilities such as hardware-based encryption (FIPS 140-2 Level 4 HSMs with KYOK) and early access to accelerators (IBM was first to deploy Intel Gaudi 3 and is delivering one of the largest AMD MI300X GPU clusters for generative AI training) provide technical advantages for this implementation. This technical leadership reinforces why IBM Cloud is the appropriate choice to accelerate LRI's biomedical discoveries in a secure, compliant, and adaptable manner.
